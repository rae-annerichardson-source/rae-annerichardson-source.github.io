Background

How I work.

I came up through advertising — copywriting, creative pods, brand campaigns for Diageo, Coca-Cola, Unilever and UNICEF — and then took that craft into energy and logistics, where the buyer is technical, the sales cycle is long, and the audience sits across a dozen jurisdictions. The combination is the point. A campaign has to do more than look good: it has to open a market, shorten a pipeline, or make a sales team faster.

In practice that means running integrated programmes end to end — go-to-market strategy, brand positioning, PR, performance marketing and sales enablement — with a measurement layer attached from the start. I currently lead a cross-functional team of five specialists across six international offices, govern a US$250K annual media budget, and write on AI and global trade for Forbes.

Roughly five years agency-side and five in-house, which means I can write the line and defend the media plan. Three advertising awards came from the creative work; US$9M in first-year revenue and a US$300M pipeline came from the commercial side. I don't think those are separate skills.
